Nature and Environment

Forests

Forests are the lungs of our planet, providing oxygen, absorbing carbon dioxide, and serving as a habitat for countless species. They house about 80% of terrestrial biodiversity, including rare and endangered species. Biodiversity in forests ensures ecosystem stability.

Oceans

Oceans cover 71% of Earth’s surface and are vital for regulating climate, supporting marine life, and providing resources for billions of people. Unfortunately, marine ecosystems are under threat from pollution, overfishing, and climate change. Plastic pollution has become a global crisis.

Climate Change

Climate change is one of the most pressing issues of our time. The burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial activities have led to a significant rise in global temperatures, with the planet warming by approximately 1.1°C since the pre-industrial era. This warming has caused melting glaciers, rising sea levels, and extreme weather events .
